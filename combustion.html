Investigating how the efficiencies of alcohols and petroleum fuels vary in terms of the enthalpy change of combustion ?

In an energy dependant era, fuels of all forms are being increasingly demanded particularly petroleum fuels and alcohols. I have lived in India, a developing country for seventeen years and I have noticed that people often use mixtures of an alcohol and petroleum fuels as sources of fuel. They have become more relevant and more easily available in developing countries due to expenses and the lack of large amounts of petroleum fuels. This investigation aims to analyse why certain fuels are used over others by determining the enthalpy change of combustion of the fuels used. New Delhi has rapidly become one of the most polluted cities in the world due to its high levels of fuel consumption and I hope to establish which fuels are the most efficient through the investigation. By applying my knowledge chemistry I aim to understand how the efficiencies of alcohols and petroleum fuels vary by determining the enthalpy change of combustion.

Organic alcohols are defined by the general statement: CnH2n+1 OH, where n= number of carbon atoms in the homologous series of alcohols. This investigation will analyse four different organic alcohols, methanol (CH3 OH), ethanol (C2H5 OH), propan-1-ol (C3H7 OH) and butan-1-ol (C4H9 OH). As the number of carbon atoms in the chain increase the strength of the hydrogen bonds will begin to rise. Therefore, the increase in bond strength with carbon chain length will have a direct impact on the enthalpy of combustion of these fuels.

This investigation proves that gasoline and diesel, the two petroleum fuels are much more efficient than the entire set of alcohols in terms of the enthalpy change of combustion. However, diesel and gasoline clearly produced large amounts of soot and pollutants. Furthermore, if this incomplete combustion of these fuels were to occur, large amounts of carbon monoxide would release into the atmosphere increasing the concentration of greenhouse gasses in the atmosphere. Therefore, even though the petroleum fuels have higher enthalpy of combustions, using alcohols-petroleum fuel mixtures may result in fewer emissions while still maintaining a high enthalpy of combustion.
